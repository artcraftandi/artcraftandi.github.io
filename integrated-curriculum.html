Integrated Curriculum

 Pedagogy

  • Methods:
    Apply methods that help different kind of learners imbibe the learning process. These methods are based on an ‘hands on experience’ thus helping students make connections between the method and the concept.
  • Approaches:
    We have tried to take inspiration with all the 3 approaches for the age group namely: The Montessori Approach, The High/Scope Approach and the Reggio Emilia Approach. Not adhering to any specific one and understanding the present needs of students, teachers and academic environment an amalgamation of all the 3 approaches has been tried successfully to bring the best but at the same time catering to each child’s developmental and educational needs.
  • Impact:
    The impact of this programme is many folds
    • It enhances skills which help in better handling of processes like writing, copying for a given text, communication; verbal and non verbal, drawing and creativity
    • It also sharpens the student’s observational and analytical ability and can help in readiness for school processes

 Conceptualization

  • A Learning Tool,
    Not a Teaching Tool: The programme is attached to the school syllabus and curriculum and thus helps a child give meaning to the learning process on an individual basis. It is an activity, deeply intertwined with the learning process of the child.
  • Process oriented and not product oriented:
    The effort a child takes to complete the activity and the skills those are sharpened during the process of implementation is of importance than the end product.
  • The Activities follow a pattern of development which help in the practicing of essential skills
  • The programme differentiates between creating a visual impression and a visual perception. A visual perception helps the child to transfer learning to a long term memory
